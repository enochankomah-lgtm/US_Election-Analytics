# 🇺🇸 UNITED STATE 2022 ELECTION ANALYTICS

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes the 2022 U.S. Election using Excel,SQL and Power BI to uncover voting patterns across states.  
It explores how socioeconomic and demographic factors—such as income, education, race, and inequality—relate to electoral outcomes.

The goal is to move beyond raw vote counts and understand **what drives voting behavior** across different regions in the United States.

---

## ❓ Business Questions

This project is guided by key analytical questions aimed at understanding voting behavior in the 2022 U.S. Election:

### 🗳️ Voting Patterns
- Which states are dominated by Democratic vs Republican voters?
- What are the vote margins across states?
- Which states fall into Strong, Lean, or Swing categories?

### 💰 Income & Economy
- How does income (median vs mean) relate to voting outcomes?
- Do higher-income states favor a particular party?
- What does income distribution reveal about political alignment?

### 🎓 Education
- How do different education levels influence voting behavior?
- Which education group contributes most to total votes?

### 📉 Inequality
- Does income inequality (Gini Index) impact voting patterns?

### 🌎 Demographics
- How does racial composition affect party preference?

### 🔍 Key Objective
- Identify how socioeconomic and demographic factors combine to influence electoral outcomes.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 🗳️ Voting Patterns
- A strong **inverse relationship** exists between Democratic and Republican vote shares, reflecting a dominant two-party system.
- The **District of Columbia** is a major outlier with extremely high Democratic support and vote margins.
- Many states fall into predictable categories (**Strong, Lean, Swing**), with only a few swing states influencing overall outcomes.

---

### 💰 Income & Voting Behavior
- Higher-income states tend to lean **Republican**, while lower-income states lean **Democratic**.
- However, several **swing states deviate** from this trend, indicating that income alone is not a decisive factor.
- The gap between **mean and median income** highlights uneven income distribution across states.

---

### 🎓 Education Impact
- **Medium education levels** contribute the highest share of total votes, especially for Republicans.
- A **positive correlation** exists between Democratic and Republican raw votes across education levels.
- Education influences voting patterns but does not act as a single determining factor.

---

### 📉 Income Inequality (Gini Index)
- Income inequality varies significantly across states.
- There is **no strong direct relationship** between inequality and voting preference.
- Political alignment is influenced by multiple overlapping factors, not inequality alone.

---

### 🌎 Demographics & Race
- Racial composition plays a role in voting patterns, but its influence varies by state.
- States with strong political alignment tend to have **more concentrated demographic patterns**.
- Swing states show more **balanced demographic and voting distributions**.

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- U.S. elections show a **highly polarized landscape**
- **Geography + demographics + economics** together shape voting behavior
- A small number of **swing states** remain critical in determining outcomes
- No single variable fully explains voting patterns—**multi-factor analysis is essential**
